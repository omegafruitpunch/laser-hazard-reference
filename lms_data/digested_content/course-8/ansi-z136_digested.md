## 1. Laser Beam Hazards

### Optical Hazards
- Entertainment lasers typically operate at 200mW to 40W+ (Class 3B and 4)
- Visible wavelengths (400-700nm) pose significant eye risks over 100+ meters
- Non-visible wavelengths (<400nm and >700nm) are MORE hazardous than visible
- Eye cannot react to wavelengths it cannot see

### Control Measures
| Risk | Control Measure |
|------|-----------------|
| High power lasers | Match laser power to venue size |
| Eye exposure | Use scanning failure safety systems |
| Non-visible wavelengths | Verify only visible wavelengths present |
| Scanned beam failures | Safer programming + electronic failure systems |
| Reflected beams | Assess secondary beams with irradiance measurements |
| Pulsed lasers (Q-switched) | DO NOT USE where people may be exposed |

### Skin Burns and Fire Risks
- Lasers >500mW can cause skin burns and fire
- UV radiation (<400nm) creates additional skin exposure risk
- Beams can ignite combustible materials in optical path
- **Control:** Verify visible wavelengths only, terminate on non-combustible surfaces

### Other Hazards
- **Equipment displacement:** Secure against inadvertent movement
- **Outdoor distractions:** Dazzle motorists and pilots
- **Air Navigation Order violations:** Exposure of pilots may be an offense

## 2. Managing Laser Safety

### Key Roles

#### Client
- Ensure retained personnel are competent
- Verify installation/display safety

#### Organiser
- Implement safety management processes
- Ensure communications/liaison arrangements

#### Venue
- Ensure safe installation and operation
- No risk to workers or public

#### Laser Safety Officer (LSO)
- Evaluate and manage laser risks
- Implement specified safety precautions
- Must have **EXECUTIVE** responsibility (not just advisory)

#### Laser Safety Adviser (LSA)
- Independent advisor with fundamental knowledge
- Advisory role only, no executive mandate

### Competency Requirements
- Detailed knowledge of laser systems being used
- Type, model, power, divergence, wavelengths
- Effect capabilities and exposure hazards
- Operating control systems
- Emergency shutdown procedures

### Emergency Procedures
- E-stop must require deliberate action to restart
- Test E-stop before each performance
- Actual eye injury = medical emergency
- Perceived injury (no visible symptoms):
  - If no exposure occurred: reassurance only
  - If concern remains: eye hospital within 24 hours
  - Use Amsler Grid for retinal function assessment

## 3. Show Planning and System Design

### Creative Brief Elements
- What is the creative vision?
- Where will effects be used?
- When will laser be used?
- How will it be executed?

### Location Considerations
- Personal exposure risks from fault conditions
- Target material reflectivity
- Specular vs. diffuse reflection
- Occupancy of nearby buildings/structures

### Safe Programming Techniques
1. Multiple scanning components (fail-safe design)
2. Diffraction effect safety (consider zero-order)
3. Fault-tolerant control systems
4. Scan-failure detection with auto-shutdown (< few milliseconds)
5. **Never leave audience-exposing installations unattended**

### MPE Selection Criteria

| Exposure Situation | MPE Time Base |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Audience - normal | Continuous, direct ocular, 10+ seconds |
| Audience - fault | Match safety system response time (min 10s) |
| Employees - trained | 0.25s (aversion response) for CW beams |
| Performers | Training includes choreography for risk negation |

## 4. Installation and Alignment

### Legal Requirements (UK)
- Control of Artificial Optical Radiation at Work Regulations 2010
- Control measures to reduce risk "as low as reasonably practicable"

### Separation Distances

| Installation Type | Vertical | Horizontal |
|------------------|----------|------------|
| Supervised | 3m above | 2.5m from audience |
| Unsupervised | 6m above | 2.5m from audience |

### Installation Requirements
- Warning signs for laser hazard areas
- Restrict entry to authorized personnel
- Secure external mirrors (tamper-proof, fail-safe)
- Masking to restrict reflection angles

### Alignment Safety
- Mask effects head to confine beam movement
- Materials: black-painted steel/aluminum or block board
- Software blanking should NOT be sole safety measure
- Reduce beams below MPE during alignment if possible
- Clear area of personnel during programming

### Programming and Rehearsal
- Programming ideally done prior to installation
- On-site programming: clear personnel from beam areas
- Operator vigilance during rehearsals
- Additional E-stop operator when performers exposed

## 5. Pre-Show Checks

### Visual Checks (Daily)
- Alignment of all optical elements
- Specified procedure from hand-over documentation

### Physical Checks
- Pass key or password control systems
- Control console positioned away from accessible areas
- E-stop clearly identifiable and easily operated
- Test E-stop before each performance

### MPE Compliance Verification
- Demonstrate compliance under scanning failure conditions
- Measure worst-case exposure
- Confirm calculation accuracy with measurements
- Complex installations may take several hours

### Sign-Off Documentation
- Installer confirms safe to use
- Organiser/venue confirms understanding
- Event manager and venue operators give permission
- Record checks carried out at each stage

---

## 6. Operation and Post-Show

### Monitoring the Show
- Prevent audience exposure through irresponsible behavior
- Be vigilant for equipment failure
- Stop display for unsafe conditions (audience unruliness, etc.)

### Post-Show
- Record: show went without incident OR unplanned events
- Date, time, and operator signature
- Consider dismantling risks (fatigue, darkness, time pressure)

## 7. Key Terms (Glossary Excerpts)

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Accessible Emission** | Laser radiation accessible to human eye/skin |
| **AEL** | Accessible Emission Limit - power levels for classification |
| **MPE** | Maximum Permissible Exposure - safe exposure level |
| **Scanning** | Moving laser beam using galvanometer mirrors |
| **Specular Reflection** | Mirror-like reflection maintaining beam coherence |
| **Diffuser** | Surface scattering light in many directions |
| **E-stop** | Emergency stop control |
| **SRCS** | Safety Related Control Systems |
