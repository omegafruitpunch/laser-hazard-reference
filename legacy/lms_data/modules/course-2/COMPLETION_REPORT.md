## Modules Built

### Module 2.1: FDA Regulatory Framework
**Duration:** 35 minutes | **Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Learning Objectives:**
- Navigate the FDA laser product regulatory structure under 21 CFR
- Understand the CDRH/FDA organizational relationship
- Identify key reporting requirements (Product, Supplemental, Annual Reports)
- Recognize historical evolution of FDA laser regulations
- Apply compliance timelines to real-world scenarios

**Interactive Components:**
1. **Regulatory Timeline** - Interactive timeline of FDA milestones (1960-2023)
   - 12 key historical events
   - Progressive disclosure of milestone details
   - Visual timeline with significance indicators

2. **Form Selection Wizard** - Decision tree for determining which FDA form to use
   - 5 main decision branches (New Product, Modification, Incident, Variance, Annual)
   - Multiple scenario pathways
   - Immediate result delivery with form references

**Lessons:**
- 2.1.1: The Regulatory Landscape (8 min)
- 2.1.2: CDRH and FDA Structure (8 min)
- 2.1.3: Reporting Requirements (10 min)
- 2.1.4: Compliance Challenge (9 min)

### Module 2.2: Laser Product Classification (FDA)
**Duration:** 40 minutes | **Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish between FDA laser classes (I, IIa, II, IIIa, IIIb, IV)
- Calculate accessible emission limits (AELs)
- Apply measurement parameters per 21 CFR 1040.10(e)
- Identify required controls for each class
- Determine classification based on specifications

**Interactive Components:**
1. **Classification Flowchart** - Decision tree for Class I-IV determination
   - Wavelength-based routing
   - Duration and power checks
   - Step-by-step classification logic

2. **AEL Calculator** - Interactive calculator for emission limits
   - Wavelength, duration, and beam diameter inputs
   - Automatic Class I-IIIa AEL calculations
   - Visual formula display

3. **Requirements Matrix** - Side-by-side comparison table
   - 9 requirement categories across 6 laser classes
   - Interactive filtering
   - Direct CFR references

**Lessons:**
- 2.2.1: Understanding FDA Classes (10 min)
- 2.2.2: Accessible Emission Limits (10 min)
- 2.2.3: Classification Decision Tree (10 min)
- 2.2.4: Requirements by Class (10 min)

**Mastery Badge:** Classification Expert

## Pedagogical Implementation

### Design Patterns Applied (from Brilliant.org research)
1. **4-Phase Structure:** Warm-up → Core Concept → Guided Practice → Challenges
2. **Progressive Disclosure:** Complex regulatory text revealed in layers
3. **Immediate Feedback:** All interactions provide instant response
4. **Single Concept Focus:** Each lesson targets one specific competency

### Adult Learning Principles (from Andragogy research)
1. **Need to Know:** Opening hooks explain "Why this matters"
2. **Self-Directed:** Learners navigate at their own pace
3. **Experience-Based:** Case studies from real FDA actions
4. **Problem-Centered:** Branching scenarios for compliance decisions
5. **Relevance:** Connects to actual regulatory obligations

### Interactive Design Patterns (from research)
1. **Branching Scenarios:** Compliance decision-making simulations
2. **Drag-and-Drop:** Requirements matching exercises
3. **Progressive Calculation:** Step-by-step AEL calculations
4. **Visual Proofs:** Interactive diagrams for concepts
