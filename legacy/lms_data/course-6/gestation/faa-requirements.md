# FAA Requirements for Outdoor Laser Operations

## Source: FAA AC 70-1B & Form 7140-1

---

## 1. Regulatory Authority

### Legal Basis
- **Title 49 U.S.C. Section 40103**: Sovereignty and Use of Airspace
- **Public Right of Transit**: FAA authority to regulate navigable airspace
- **49 U.S.C., Subtitle VII, Part A**: Air Commerce and Safety
- **49 U.S.C., Subtitle VII, Part B**: Airport Development and Noise

### Key FAA Documents
- **FAA Advisory Circular 70-1B**: Outdoor Laser Operations (Current: Feb 14, 2022)
- **FAA Order JO 7400.2, Chapter 29**: Procedures for Handling Airspace Matters - Outdoor Laser Operations
- **FAA Advisory Circular 70-2**: Reporting of Laser Illumination of Aircraft
- **FAA Form 7140-1**: Notice of Proposed Outdoor Laser Operation(s)

## 2. Notification Thresholds

### When FAA Notification is REQUIRED

Submit FAA Form 7140-1 when:

| Laser Type | Threshold Condition |
|------------|---------------------|
| **Visible lasers (400-700 nm)** | Beam exceeds **50 nW/cm²** in navigable airspace |
| **Any laser (visible or non-visible)** | Exceeds **Maximum Permissible Exposure (MPE)** in navigable airspace |

### Navigable Airspace Definition
Airspace above the minimum altitudes of flight including:
- Airspace needed for aircraft takeoff/landing
- Airspace required for safe aircraft operations
- All altitudes where aircraft normally operate

### Exemptions
- Indoor laser operations (fully enclosed)
- Lasers with beams completely contained and terminated
- Operations below navigable airspace minimums with no aircraft access

## 3. FAA Form 7140-1 Components

### Section 1: General Information
- FAA Service Center identification
- Proponent name and contact information
- Event/facility name
- Date prepared
- Customer and site address

### Section 2: Date(s) and Time(s)
- Testing and alignment dates/times
- Operation dates/times
- Time zone specification

### Section 3: Laser Operation Description
- General overview of operation
- Specific laser configurations

### Section 4: On-site Operation Information
- Operator names and titles
- Primary and secondary on-site phone numbers

### Section 5: FDA/CDRH Information
- Variance number (if applicable)
- Variance expiration date
- Accession number

### Section 6: Control Measures
- Termination methods
- Observer procedures
- Electronic protection systems
- Physical limiting methods

### Section 7: Attachments
- Number of laser configurations
- Supporting documentation list

### Section 8: Designated Contact Person
- Name, position, phone, fax, email
- Must be most knowledgeable about laser safety

### Section 9: Statement of Accuracy
- Signature attesting to accuracy of information

### Section 10-14: Laser Configuration Worksheet
- Configuration number and description
- Geographic location (elevation, GPS coordinates)
- Beam characteristics and calculations
- Protection distances (NOHD, SZED, CZED, LFED)

## 4. FAA Review Process

### Steps After Submission

1. **Initial Review**: FAA Service Center reviews submitted information
2. **Follow-up Questions**: FAA contacts proponent if clarification needed
3. **Aeronautical Study**: Coordination with affected aviation parties
4. **Letter of Determination (LOD)**: FAA issues determination

### Possible FAA Determinations

| Determination | Meaning |
|---------------|---------|
| **No Objection** | FAA has no objection to proposed laser use |
| **Objection** | FAA objects with explanation; proponent may modify and resubmit |

### Processing Time
- Estimated: **240 minutes per response** (including preparation)
- OMB Control Number: **2120-0662**
- Expiration Date: **07/31/2024** (as of document date)

## 6. Key Compliance Points

### Pre-Operation Requirements
1. ✓ Submit FAA Form 7140-1 to appropriate Service Center
2. ✓ Receive FAA Letter of Determination (no objection)
3. ✓ Obtain FDA variance if required
4. ✓ Implement control measures as described
5. ✓ Coordinate with local authorities (does not supersede local regulations)

### During Operation
- Maintain on-site communication capability
- Follow approved laser configurations
- Implement control measures as documented
- Be prepared for FAA contact

### Electronic Protection Systems
If using electronic protection instead of safety observers:
- Must meet **SAE AS6029** performance criteria
- Provide additional documentation certifying compliance
- Describe system in Section 6 of Form 7140-1

---

## 7. Service Center Contact Information

FAA has multiple Service Centers; submit to the center responsible for the geographic area of operations. See Appendix E of AC 70-1B for current contact details.
